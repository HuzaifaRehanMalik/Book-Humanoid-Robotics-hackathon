# APA Citation Guidelines for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook

## General APA Format

For this textbook, all citations must follow the 7th edition of the American Psychological Association (APA) style guide. This ensures consistency and academic rigor throughout the content.

## In-Text Citations

### Single Author
- Parenthetical: (Author, Year) - e.g., (Smith, 2023)
- Narrative: Author (Year) - e.g., Smith (2023) states that...

### Multiple Authors
- Two authors: (Author1 & Author2, Year) - e.g., (Smith & Jones, 2023)
- Three to 20 authors: (Author1 et al., Year) - e.g., (Smith et al., 2023)
- More than 21 authors: List first 19, then ellipsis, then last author

### No Date
- Use (Author, n.d.) when no date is available

## Reference List Format

### Books
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle*. Publisher.

Example:
Brooks, R. A. (2022). *Flesh and machines: How robots will change us*. Vintage Books.

### Journal Articles
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Periodical*, volume(issue), pages. https://doi.org/xx.xxx/yyyy

Example:
Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (2023). Robotics: Science and systems. *Annual Review of Control, Robotics, and Autonomous Systems*, 6(1), 1-25. https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-control-080521-021309

### Conference Papers
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year, Month). Title of paper. In *Proceedings of Conference Name* (pp. page range). Publisher. DOI or URL

Example:
Kaelbling, L. P., & Lozano-PÃ©rez, T. (2023, May). Hierarchical task networks for long-horizon manipulation. In *Proceedings of the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation* (pp. 1234-1241). IEEE. https://doi.org/10.1109/ICRA48891.2023.10162405

### Websites
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Date). Title of webpage. *Website Name*. URL

Example:
OpenAI. (2023, June 13). GPT-4 technical report. https://openai.com/research/gpt-4

### Reports
Institution Name. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. if applicable). URL

Example:
National Science Foundation. (2023). *Robotics roadmap: From internet to robotics* (NSF Report No. 23-101). https://www.nsf.gov/reports/robotics-roadmap

## Special Considerations for Robotics Textbook

### Technical Papers and Preprints
When citing technical papers from repositories like arXiv:
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). *Title of paper* [Preprint]. arXiv. URL

Example:
Lillicrap, T. P., et al. (2023). Continuous control with deep reinforcement learning [Preprint]. arXiv. https://arxiv.org/abs/1509.02971

### Software and Libraries
For software and libraries:
Author, A. A. [Username]. (Year). *Software name* (Version number) [Computer software]. Repository/URL

Example:
Robot Operating System. (2023). *ROS 2* (Humble Hawksbill) [Computer software]. https://github.com/ros2/ros2

## Citation Examples in Textbook Context

### When citing foundational robotics concepts:
The potential field method, originally developed by Khatib (1986), remains a fundamental approach for robot navigation.

### When citing recent research:
Recent advances in reinforcement learning for robotics have shown promising results (Levine et al., 2023).

### When citing textbooks for foundational knowledge:
For a comprehensive overview of robot kinematics, readers are referred to the foundational work by Craig (2005).

## Notes
- Always include DOI when available
- Use sentence case for article titles, title case for journal names
- For online sources, include retrieval date if the content is likely to change over time
- When citing multiple sources for a single statement, order them chronologically or alphabetically by first author's surname
- For direct quotes, always include page or paragraph numbers (Author, Year, p. #) or (Author, Year, para. #) for online sources without page numbers

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Missing DOI or URL for online sources
- Incorrect capitalization in titles
- Missing publisher information
- Inconsistent formatting between references
- Mixing different citation styles within the same document
